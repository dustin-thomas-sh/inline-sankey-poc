Inline Sankey Diagrams

Word-sized flow visualizations embedded directly in text — proof of concept

What Is This?

Edward Tufte coined the term sparkline for "small, intense, word-sized graphics." An inline sankey (or "sparksankey") applies that idea to flow data — showing how quantities split and merge across stages, right inside a sentence, without breaking reading flow.

Inline Examples

Revenue Flow

Last quarter, revenue split across three channels with direct sales dominating but partnerships growing 18% QoQ.

User Funnel

Of 10,000 visitors, the conversion funnel shows most drop-off at signup, with strong retention post-activation.

Support Tickets

This week's 847 support tickets flowed as — billing issues escalate at 3× the rate of product bugs.

How This Was Built

Someone saw Elijah Meeks post about building a "sparksankey" in 15 minutes with AI and thought: "wait, can we actually do that?"

Turns out, yes. Claude researched Meeks' Semiotic framework (which had a dedicated SparkNetworkFrame), dug through the d3-sankey docs, and then built this entire page — from concept to working prototype — in a single conversation. The whole thing is one self-contained HTML file. No build tools, no frameworks, no npm install. Just vibes and CDN links.

Each inline sankey is a tiny <svg> with display: inline-block, laid out by d3-sankey, and sized to match your text's line-height. Hover any node or link for details. That's it. That's the whole trick.
